Cyber Security Facts

  1. Cyber Threats are Diverse: Cyber threats come in various forms, including malware, phishing attacks, ransomware, DDoS (Distributed Denial of Service) attacks, and more. Each type of threat requires a specific approach for prevention.

  2. Protecting Information: Internet security is like a digital shield that helps keep our personal information safe when we use the internet.

  3. Strong Passwords Matter: Just like a secret code, having strong and unique passwords for our online accounts helps keep them secure.

  4. Locking the Virtual Door: It's important to use secure websites (those with "https://" in the address) to make sure our online activities are safe and private.

  5. Beware of Tricky Messages: Sometimes, people online may try to trick us with fake messages or emails. We should always check with a trusted adult if something seems suspicious.

  6. Updates Keep us Safe: Just like our toys get new features, our devices need updates to fix bugs and stay safe from online bugs. Always keep your devices updated.

  7. Think Before Clicking: Before clicking on links or downloading things online, it's smart to double-check and make sure they're from trusted sources. This helps avoid nasty surprises

  8. Cyber Friends Need Caution: Online friends may not always be who they say they are. It's important to only chat with people we know in real life and to tell a trusted adult if someone online makes us uncomfortable.

  9. Ask for Help: If something online seems confusing or if we're not sure what to do, it's okay to ask a parent, teacher, or another trusted adult for help. They can guide us to make safe choices on the internet.
